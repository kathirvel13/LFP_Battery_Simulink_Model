# Lithium-Ion Batteries

A **Lithium-ion battery** is a rechargeable electrochemical energy storage device where lithium ions move between the **anode (usually graphite)** and **cathode (lithium metal oxide)** during charge/discharge.

* **Charging:** Li⁺ ions move from cathode → anode  
* **Discharging:** Li⁺ ions move from anode → cathode

# Properties of Lithium-Ion Batteries

### LiFePO₄ (Lithium Iron Phosphate)

Longer life, Higher usable capacity, Better efficiency

* High DoD (Depth of Discharge) \- 90%  
* Number of charging/discharging cycles  
* Stable chemistry (safe for rural deployment)  
* Long cycle life (2000–6000+ cycles)  
* High usable capacity (80–90% DoD)  
* Works well with BMS \+ data collection  
* Industry standard for **microgrids & solar storage**

### Nominal Voltage:

* 12.8V (single module) (4S)  
* 48V systems common (51.2V nominal) (standard for solar microgrids) (15S)

### Capacity:

* 50 Ah – 200 Ah (modular)  
* 5 kWh, 10 kWh packs common

# Lithium Iron Phosphate (LFP / LiFePO₄)

### Key Properties:

* Voltage: \~3.2V/cell  
* Energy density: 90–160 Wh/kg  
* Cycle life: **2000–6000+ cycles**  
* Thermal stability: Very high (safe)  
* Cost: Moderate

### Parameters:

* Depth of Discharge (DoD): up to 90–100%  
* Efficiency: \~95–98%  
* Operating temp: \-20°C to 60°C

## Key Parameters

| Parameter | Meaning | Typical Value (LFP) |
| ----- | ----- | ----- |
| **Energy Density (Wh/kg)** | How much energy per weight | 90–160 Wh/kg |
| **Power Density (W/kg)** | How fast energy can be delivered | 250–1000 W/kg |
| **Cycle Life** | Number of charge/discharge cycles | 2000–6000+ cycles |
| **Depth of Discharge (DoD)** | Usable capacity (%) | 80–100% |
| **State of Charge (SoC)** | Current charge level | 0–100% |
| **State of Health (SoH)** | Battery aging condition | \>80% (usable) |
| **Efficiency (%)** | Charge-discharge efficiency | 95–98% |
| **C-rate** | Charge/discharge speed | 0.5C–1C typical |
| **Thermal Stability** | Safety under heat | Very high |
| **Nominal Voltage** | Average operating voltage | 3.2V per cell |

# Key Battery Parameters

Assume: 12.8V LFP battery, 100Ah

| Parameter | Explanation | Ideal Value (LFP 12V) |
| ----- | ----- | ----- |
| **Voltage** | Electrical potential of battery | 12.8V nominal (10–14.6V range) |
| **Current** | Flow of charge | Depends on load (e.g., 10–50A typical) |
| **Energy** | Total stored energy | \~1280 Wh (12.8V × 100Ah) |
| **Power** | Rate of energy usage | Depends on load (e.g., 500W–1kW) |
| **Temperature** | Operating condition | 15°C – 35°C ideal |
| **SoC** | Charge level (%) | Operate between 20–90% |
| **SoH** | Battery health | \>90% good, \<80% aging |
| **DoD** | % of capacity used | Up to 80–90% daily |
| **Lifetime** | Total usable years | 8–15 years |
| **C-rating** | Charge/discharge rate | 0.5C ideal (50A for 100Ah) |
| **Cycle Counting** | Number of cycles used | Track up to \~3000+ |
| **RUL (Remaining Useful Life)** | Time before failure | Estimated via SoH \+ cycles |
| **Internal Resistance** | Opposition to current flow | Very low (\~2–10 mΩ) |
